# I am creating a discord bot and I want it to be able to sit there 24/7 and aggregate data. How do I do this without hosting it myself?

Source: [Perplexity thread](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/72ab37d8-c2ce-4687-b2a1-d3d6dddb73cf)

## User

I am creating a discord bot and I want it to be able to sit there 24/7 and aggregate data. How do I do this without hosting it myself?

## Assistant

To host your Discord bot 24/7 without managing the hosting yourself, you can use third-party hosting services. These platforms allow you to keep your bot running continuously without needing to maintain your own hardware or server. Here are some of the best options, both free and paid:

### **Free Hosting Options**
1. **Discloud**: This platform offers free hosting for small bots and is easy to set up. However, it may have limitations in terms of uptime and performance for larger or more resource-intensive bots.[3]
2. **Railway**: Railway provides a free tier that is sufficient for most small bots. It’s relatively user-friendly and offers a simple setup process.[3]
3. **Bot-Hosting.net**: A free service specifically designed for Discord bots, offering easy setup and 24/7 availability. However, as with most free services, it may have limitations in terms of performance and support.[6]

### **Paid Hosting Options**
1. **SparkedHost**: Known for its affordability, SparkedHost offers plans starting at just $1 per month, making it a great choice for budget-conscious users who want reliable uptime and performance.[4][7]
2. **BisectHosting**: This platform offers flexible pricing with plans starting at $1.19 per month. It has worldwide server locations and is optimized for 24/7 uptime with DDoS protection.[5][4]
3. **PebbleHost**: Another affordable option, PebbleHost offers strong protection and high performance with plans starting from $3 per month. It’s beginner-friendly and provides good support for scaling your bot.[7][4]
4. **Vultr**: Vultr is ideal for users who want more control over their hosting environment. It offers VPS hosting starting at $2.50 per month, which is perfect if you need more customization options.[4]

### **Other Notable Options**
- **AWS Free Tier**: AWS provides a free tier for the first 12 months, which could be a good option if you’re comfortable with Linux and server management. After the free period, costs start around $8.50 per month.[2]
- **Replit**: While Replit allows you to host Discord bots, it has limitations like rate-limiting and performance issues that make it less ideal for long-term hosting.[3]

### **Conclusion**
For reliable 24/7 hosting without self-hosting, paid services like SparkedHost or BisectHosting offer affordable solutions with good performance and security features. If you're looking for a free option, Discloud or Railway can work well for smaller bots but may have some limitations in uptime or performance.
