# Is it really not as simple as creating a general-purpose anything-prompt for perplexity that can convert a google search into a perplexity prompt?

Given my understanding, it **seems** AI can write prompts much better than humans, when they are prompted to do so. For example, I can ask chatgpt to write a prompt I can use in perplexity.

So what reasoning is there for continually playing a game of telephone, e.g. tasking one AI to write a prompt for another AI that writes the specialized prompt for the final Perplexity prompt.

Source: [Perplexity thread](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/1b634f38-0125-4f29-b75f-947429e0aa5c)

## User

Is it really not as simple as creating a general-purpose anything-prompt for perplexity that can convert a google search into a perplexity prompt?

Given my understanding, it **seems** AI can write prompts much better than humans, when they are prompted to do so. For example, I can ask chatgpt to write a prompt I can use in perplexity.

So what reasoning is there for continually playing a game of telephone, e.g. tasking one AI to write a prompt for another AI that writes the specialized prompt for the final Perplexity prompt.

## Assistant

While it's true that AI models like ChatGPT can generate prompts for other AI systems, creating a single, all-purpose prompt for Perplexity that can effectively convert any Google search into an optimal Perplexity prompt is not as simple as it might seem. Here's why:

1. Query complexity: Search queries can vary greatly in complexity, context, and intent. A one-size-fits-all approach may not capture the nuances of every possible query.[1]

2. Domain-specific knowledge: Different topics require different levels of specificity and context. A general-purpose prompt might not provide the necessary depth for specialized queries.[1]

3. Perplexity's unique features: Perplexity has specific capabilities and limitations that differ from other search engines. A general prompt might not fully leverage Perplexity's strengths or account for its constraints.[2]

4. Evolving AI capabilities: As AI models improve, their ability to understand and respond to prompts changes. A static, general-purpose prompt might become less effective over time.[3]

5. User intent interpretation: Accurately interpreting the user's intent from a Google search query and translating it into an effective Perplexity prompt requires contextual understanding that may be challenging for a single, general-purpose prompt.[4]

6. Iterative refinement: Effective prompting often involves an iterative process of refining queries based on initial results, which is difficult to encapsulate in a single, static prompt.[5]

While using AI to generate prompts for other AI systems can be helpful, it's not a perfect solution. The "game of telephone" you described can actually be beneficial in some cases, as each step allows for refinement and specialization of the prompt. However, it's important to note that this process can also introduce errors or misinterpretations at each stage.

Ultimately, the most effective approach often involves a combination of AI-generated prompts and human oversight, allowing for the benefits of AI efficiency while maintaining the nuanced understanding and adaptability that human users can provide.
