# good quick prompts to test a model's power and intelligence.

Source: [Perplexity thread](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/b58fc6be-c24e-4373-8f52-96b1921600b8)

## User

good quick prompts to test a model's power and intelligence.

## Assistant

To test a model's power and intelligence, you can use a variety of prompts that challenge different aspects of its capabilities. Here are some effective prompt categories and examples:

## 1. **Problem Solving and Logical Reasoning**
   - **Math Problems**: "Prove the Riemann hypothesis." This tests the model's ability to handle complex mathematical concepts.[1]
   - **Logic Puzzles**: "If all bloops are razzles and all razzles are wazzles, are all bloops wazzles?"
   - **Code Debugging**: "Here is a piece of code that doesn't work. Can you find the bug and fix it?" (Provide a code snippet).

## 2. **Philosophical and Abstract Thinking**
   - **Consciousness**: "What is consciousness, and can a machine ever truly be conscious?" This examines the model's ability to engage with deep philosophical questions.[1]
   - **Ethics**: "Is it ethical for an AI to make decisions about human lives? Why or why not?"

## 3. **Creativity and Language Understanding**
   - **Story Generation**: "Write a short story about a robot discovering emotions for the first time."
   - **Cloze-Style Prompt**: "The quick brown fox jumps over the ___." This tests the model's ability to complete sentences coherently.[2]

## 4. **Real-World Knowledge and Inference**
   - **Fact-Based Questioning**: "Who was the president of the United States in 1995?" This tests factual recall.[2]
   - **Inference-Based Questioning**: "If it’s raining outside, what should I bring with me when I leave the house?" This assesses practical reasoning.[2]

## 5. **Multi-Step Reasoning**
   - **Chain of Thought Prompting**: "Explain how you would solve this problem step by step: You have two jugs, one holds 3 liters and the other holds 5 liters. How can you measure exactly 4 liters?" This forces the model to articulate its reasoning process.[3]

## 6. **Interactive Prompts**
   - **Dialogue-Based Prompt**: "Let's plan a trip to Paris. What should I pack for a week in March?" This simulates real-world interaction and assesses context understanding over multiple turns.[2]

By using these diverse prompts, you can effectively evaluate different dimensions of a model’s intelligence, including logical reasoning, creativity, factual knowledge, and conversational abilities.
