# What are the most common complaints about HAOS from users

Source: [Perplexity thread](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/f15e9fd6-595f-4820-a100-6fb8ebc4e554)

## User

What are the most common complaints about HAOS from users

## Assistant

Users of Home Assistant Operating System (HAOS) have voiced several common complaints across various platforms. These issues range from technical bugs to frustrations with documentation and community support. Below are the most frequent complaints:

## **1. Bluetooth and Hardware Issues**
One of the most commonly reported problems involves Bluetooth functionality, particularly after upgrading to HAOS 10. Users running HAOS on Raspberry Pi and Intel NUCs have reported that their Bluetooth adapters stop working intermittently, causing integrations to fail. Many users have had to revert to older versions, such as HAOS 9.5, to restore Bluetooth functionality. This issue has persisted across multiple updates, leading to frustration among users who rely on Bluetooth for home automation devices.[1]

## **2. Update-Related Breakages**
Several users have reported that updates often lead to system instability or even complete failures. For example, after upgrading to HAOS 13.1, some users experienced boot loops or bricked installations on Raspberry Pi devices. This problem is particularly concerning for those who use HAOS for critical home automation tasks, as these breakages can disrupt entire smart home setups.[5]

## **3. Documentation Frustrations**
New users frequently express dissatisfaction with Home Assistant's documentation. Complaints include the lack of clear, step-by-step guides and examples, especially for complex tasks like scripting and custom integrations. Users have noted that many documentation pages end abruptly or lack sufficient detail, leaving them to guess the next steps. Additionally, there is frustration over outdated syntax examples in the documentation, which can confuse users when newer versions of Home Assistant introduce changes.[3]

## **4. DNS Configuration Problems**
Another technical issue involves DNS settings in HAOS. Users have complained about forced DNS configurations that override local DNS settings, breaking functionality in environments where a local DNS server is present. This issue has been exacerbated by what some perceive as dismissive responses from developers when users raise concerns about the problem.[4]

## **5. Community and Support Issues**
Some users have expressed frustration with the Home Assistant community and support channels. Newcomers often feel that their questions are met with hostility or unhelpful responses from more experienced members or developers. This has led to a perception that Home Assistant is difficult to approach for those unfamiliar with its intricacies.[3]

In summary, the most common complaints about HAOS include:
- Bluetooth failures after updates.
- System instability or boot failures following upgrades.
- Poor or incomplete documentation.
- Forced DNS configurations that break local networks.
- Unwelcoming community support for new users.

These issues highlight a balance between adding new features and ensuring stability and usability for both new and experienced users alike.
