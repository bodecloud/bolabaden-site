## Assistant

Denver's population dynamics are complex, with both growth and decline occurring depending on the area and timeframe considered.

### **Growth in the Denver Metro Area**
The broader Denver-Aurora-Centennial metropolitan area has been growing steadily. Between July 2020 and July 2023, the metro area saw a 1.2% population increase, adding over 35,000 new residents. This growth is driven by people moving to the region, making Denver one of the fastest-growing large cities in the U.S., ranking 22nd in net migration between 2021 and 2022. However, this growth rate is slower than some other metro areas, particularly in the South.[1][3]

### **Population Decline in Denver County**
Despite the overall metro area growth, Denver County itself has experienced a slight population decline. From 2020 to 2024, Denver County's population decreased by approximately 0.19%, with an annual decline rate of -0.05%. This trend is attributed to several factors:[2]
- **Affordability Issues**: High housing costs have made it difficult for many people to afford living in Denver, prompting some to relocate to more affordable regions.[4]
- **Declining Birth Rates**: Denver County has seen a significant drop in birth rates, with a 6.3% decline in births from 2021 to 2022, contributing to the overall population decrease.[7]
- **Migration Patterns**: While many people are moving to Denver from other states and countries, there has also been an outflow of residents due to affordability concerns and job opportunities elsewhere.[4]

### **Impact of Migration**
Denver has also been a major destination for migrants arriving from the U.S.-Mexico border. Over the past year, nearly 40,000 migrants have arrived in the city, which has placed additional pressure on public services like housing and healthcare. While many migrants move on to other cities, a significant portion stays in Denver, contributing to population fluctuations.[5][8]

### **Conclusion**
Overall, while Denver's metropolitan area continues to grow modestly, Denver County itself is experiencing slight population declines due to high living costs and lower birth rates. The city's future growth will likely depend on addressing these affordability issues and managing the influx of new residents effectively.
