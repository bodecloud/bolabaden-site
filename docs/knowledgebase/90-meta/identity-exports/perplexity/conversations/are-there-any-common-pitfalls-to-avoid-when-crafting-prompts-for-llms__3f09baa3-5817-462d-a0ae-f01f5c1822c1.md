# Are there any common pitfalls to avoid when crafting prompts for LLMs

Source: [Perplexity thread](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/7f571c1c-be69-400b-82ab-3159e3c63a26)

## User

Are there any common pitfalls to avoid when crafting prompts for LLMs

## Assistant

When crafting prompts for large language models (LLMs), there are several common pitfalls to avoid in order to get the best results. Here are some key mistakes to watch out for:

## Lack of Clarity and Specificity

One of the most frequent errors is using vague or ambiguous language in prompts. LLMs thrive on specificity, so unclear instructions can lead to irrelevant or unfocused responses.[1][2]

Instead of a broad prompt like "Tell me about artificial intelligence", it's better to ask something more targeted like "Explain the main differences between supervised and unsupervised learning in AI".[1]

## Providing Insufficient Context 

Failing to provide adequate context is another common mistake. LLMs require relevant background information to generate appropriate responses. Without proper context, the model may produce generic or unrelated answers.[2][3]

For example, rather than asking "What time is high tide?", a more effective prompt would be "What times are high tides in Gloucester Harbor, Massachusetts, on August 31, 2023?"[2]

## Overloading the Prompt

While context is important, overloading the prompt with excessive information can confuse the model. Aim for a balance between providing necessary details and keeping the prompt concise.[4][2]

## Using Ambiguous or Contradictory Language

Prompts containing conflicting terms or instructions can lead to inconsistent outputs. For instance, using both "detailed" and "summary" in the same prompt may confuse the model about the desired level of detail.[2]

## Ignoring Model Limitations

It's crucial to understand the capabilities and limitations of the specific LLM you're using. Expecting the model to perform tasks beyond its training or design can lead to frustration and poor results.[4]

## Not Iterating on Prompts

Many users expect perfect results on the first try. However, effective prompt engineering often involves an iterative process of refining prompts based on initial outputs.[4]

## Using Negative Language

Phrasing prompts in terms of what not to do can be less effective than stating what you want directly. For example, instead of "Don't include information about X", try "Focus only on Y and Z".[5]

## Neglecting Formatting and Structure

The structure and format of your prompt can significantly impact the output. Using appropriate headers, lists, and other formatting elements can help guide the model to produce more organized and relevant responses.[1][2]

By avoiding these common pitfalls and following best practices for prompt engineering, you can significantly improve the quality and relevance of the outputs you receive from LLMs.
