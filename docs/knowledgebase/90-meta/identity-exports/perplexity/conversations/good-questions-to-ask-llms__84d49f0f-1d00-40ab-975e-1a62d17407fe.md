# good questions to ask LLMs

## Assistant

When interacting with Large Language Models (LLMs), asking the right questions can significantly improve the quality of the responses you receive. Here are some **good questions** and techniques to ask LLMs, based on prompt engineering principles, to get insightful and useful answers.

## **Types of Questions to Ask LLMs**

### **1. Clarification and Contextual Questions**
LLMs perform better when they are provided with clear context and specific details. Asking questions that provide or request clarification can help refine the model's output.

- *"Can you explain [concept] in simple terms?"* – This is useful for breaking down complex topics.
- *"What additional information do you need to better answer this question?"* – This encourages the model to identify gaps in the input and ask for more details.
- *"What assumptions are you making in your answer?"* – This helps uncover any implicit assumptions in the model’s response.

### **2. Step-by-Step Reasoning Questions**
LLMs often benefit from being guided through logical steps, especially for complex tasks such as problem-solving or decision-making.

- *"Can you walk me through the steps involved in solving [problem]?"* – This prompts the model to break down its reasoning process.
- *"What are the pros and cons of [option 1] vs. [option 2]?"* – Asking for comparisons forces the model to evaluate different perspectives step by step.
- *"Can you explain your reasoning for this answer?"* – This encourages a more transparent response, revealing how the model arrived at its conclusion.

### **3. Role-based or Persona-specific Questions**
Assigning a role to the LLM can help tailor its response to fit a specific context or audience.

- *"As an expert in [field], how would you approach [problem]?"* – This leverages the model’s knowledge by framing it as an expert.
- *"If you were a teacher explaining this to a beginner, how would you explain it?"* – This helps simplify complex topics for easier understanding.
  
### **4. Hypothetical and Scenario-based Questions**
LLMs can generate creative responses when given hypothetical situations or scenarios.

- *"What would happen if [hypothetical scenario] occurred?"* – This encourages speculative thinking and creative problem-solving.
- *"Imagine you're tasked with designing a solution for [problem]. What approach would you take?"* – This pushes the model to think critically and innovatively.

### **5. Comparative or Analytical Questions**
These types of questions ask the LLM to evaluate different options or compare concepts, which can lead to more nuanced answers.

- *"How does [concept A] differ from [concept B]?"* – This is useful for understanding distinctions between similar ideas.
- *"Which approach is better for [task], and why?"* – Asking for a comparison helps the LLM weigh different factors before responding.

## **Prompt Engineering Techniques**

### **1. Be Clear and Specific**
Good prompts follow two basic principles: clarity and specificity. Avoid vague language and provide enough context so that the LLM understands exactly what you're asking.

- Example: Instead of asking, *“Who won the election?”*, ask, *“Which party won the 2023 general election in Paraguay?”*.[2]

### **2. Use Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Prompting**
This technique encourages LLMs to break down their thought processes into smaller steps, improving accuracy in complex reasoning tasks.

- Example: Ask, *“Think step by step: how would you solve this math problem?”*.[3]

### **3. Provide Examples (Few-shot Prompting)**
Providing examples within your question helps guide the LLM toward a desired pattern of response.

- Example: If asking for title ideas, provide sample titles like, *“Here are some examples: ‘Long Walk to Freedom,’ ‘Wishful Drinking.’ Now generate similar titles”*.[2]

### **4. Set Constraints or Guidelines**
Explicitly stating constraints (such as word count or tone) can help guide the LLM’s output more effectively.

- Example: Ask, *“Write a detailed explanation of quantum computing in under 200 words”*.[4]

### **5. Use Delimiters and Structure**
Using formatting techniques like bullet points, line breaks, or clear delimiters can help structure both your prompt and the model’s output more clearly.

- Example: Start your prompt with `###Instruction###` followed by clear sections like `###Question###` or `###Example###`.[3]

## **Conclusion**

To get useful answers from LLMs, it’s important to ask thoughtful questions that guide their responses effectively. By using strategies such as providing context, encouraging step-by-step reasoning, assigning roles, and being specific in your queries, you can significantly improve the quality of interactions with these models.
