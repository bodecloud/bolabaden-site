# What are the main criticisms of CrewAI from users

Source: [Perplexity thread](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/381b7552-11c5-4a61-8bc8-5fdd77c43df4)

## Assistant

The main criticisms of CrewAI, as gathered from user feedback and reviews, encompass several areas:

1. **User Experience on Windows**: Users have reported significant difficulty in using CrewAI on Windows, describing it as "useless" and a "pain in the ass to use" due to its apparent lack of compatibility or support for Windows environments.[1]

2. **Complexity and Learning Curve**: CrewAI is criticized for being too complex for intermediate programmers, especially when compared to its intended purpose as an easier alternative to LangChain. The setup process requires following extensive documentation, YouTube tutorials, and troubleshooting, yet still, users encounter issues.[1]

3. **Lack of Windows-Based Tutorials**: The documentation primarily focuses on macOS, leaving Windows users with fewer resources to help them set up and use CrewAI effectively.[1]

4. **AI Behavior in Applications**: In specific applications like the game *Crime Boss*, players have expressed frustration over the crew AI's suboptimal decision-making, such as standing in open fields during missions, making them easy targets for enemies. This behavior has led to mission failures attributed to AI rather than player error.[2]

5. **Integration Issues**: Users have noted problems with third-party integrations, suggesting that the platform's support for external tools and services is somewhat limited.[5]

6. **Pricing Complexity**: The pricing structure of CrewAI is mentioned as potentially confusing for new users, which might deter initial adoption or understanding of the service's costs.[5]

7. **UI/UX and Development**: While there is interest in a user interface for CrewAI, the development seems to be in progress, with users expressing impatience and a desire for more tangible updates.[3]

8. **Suitability for Production**: Critics argue that CrewAI is not suitable for production use cases due to its complexity and the potential reliability issues when used in real-world scenarios.[4]

These criticisms highlight areas where CrewAI could improve its user experience, documentation, platform support, and application performance to better meet the needs of its users.
