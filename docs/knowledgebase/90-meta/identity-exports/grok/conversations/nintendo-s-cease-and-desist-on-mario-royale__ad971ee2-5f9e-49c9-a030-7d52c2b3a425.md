In this context, he developed **Mario Royale** over months (off-and-on, ~3 weeks intensive), releasing it June 15, 2019, as a HTML5/Java browser game. Up to 75 players (capped from 100 for stability) raced ghosts through randomized Super Mario Bros. Worlds 1–3: compete for finite Mushrooms/Fire Flowers/Stars (first grabber wins it), break blocks to drop foes, kick shells for kills, or use Stars for rare direct hits. Win by axing Bowser first (top 3 survive; rest fall). Short, frantic rounds emphasized speed vs. greed; controller/keyboard support, no login.<grok:render card_id="d241de" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">
