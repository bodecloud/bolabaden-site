**Taste is highly subjective**, influenced by personal preferences for smokiness, saltiness, fat-to-meat balance, crispness after cooking, and overall savoriness. Factors such as preparation method (oven, skillet, or microwave) and specific variety (e.g., thick-cut, applewood-smoked, or hickory) also play significant roles.

### Insights from Recent Blind Taste Tests (2025–2026)
Professional evaluations by sources such as Food & Wine, Parade, Serious Eats, and others provide the following relevant observations for these widely available options:

- **Jimmy Dean** (e.g., Applewood Smoked or Hickory Smoked varieties) consistently performs well. It is frequently noted for robust, pork-forward flavor, sturdy texture with minimal shrinkage, and excellent crisp-chewy balance—earning recognition as “best for a BLT” in a 2025 Food & Wine panel and ranking as a strong runner-up for classic flavor and crispiness in other 2025 assessments.
- **Oscar Mayer** (particularly Naturally Hardwood Smoked or thick-cut) delivers reliable crispness and a balanced, nostalgic porky profile in several tests, with one panel highlighting it as the “crispiest.” However, some 2025 evaluations describe it as comparatively bland or greasy relative to bolder competitors.
- **Hormel Black Label** (original or thick-cut) receives mixed feedback. It is appreciated in certain reviews for its savory, smoky character and premium positioning, yet others note it can taste overly salty, fatty, or prone to excessive shrinkage.
- **Farmland** (classic or hickory-smoked) is often viewed favorably as an affordable, straightforward option with solid smokiness and texture, though it rarely tops rankings in head-to-head comparisons.

### Recommendation
Among the clearly displayed options, **Jimmy Dean bacon** (particularly the applewood or hickory-smoked varieties on the lower shelves) emerges as one of the stronger performers across multiple independent taste tests for overall flavor depth, texture, and versatility.

If your preference leans toward maximum crispiness and a familiar, balanced taste, **Oscar Mayer** remains a dependable classic. For thicker, more intensely flavored slices, consider the **Hormel Black Label** thick-cut options, though results vary more widely.

Ultimately, individual results may differ based on batch, storage, and cooking technique. Sampling a small package of your top two candidates is the most reliable way to determine personal preference.
