InfernoPlus, a prominent YouTuber and modder best known for extensive Halo: Combat Evolved (PC/CE) modifications such as "Cursed Halo" (featuring absurd weapon, vehicle, and enemy alterations) and other chaotic Halo projects, created Mario Royale in 2019.<grok:render card_id="2650d3" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">

</grok:render> This browser-based game transformed Super Mario Bros. The project's rapid virality—peaking at thousands of concurrent players—directly precipitated Nintendo's legal intervention. On June 21, 2019, InfernoPlus received a formal cease-and-desist (C&D) notice accompanied by DMCA takedown demands, explicitly citing copyright infringement over the use of Mario characters, sprites, music, sound effects, and level elements.<grok:render card_id="9719e7" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">

</grok:render> Consulting a lawyer, he was instructed to act within two hours to avoid escalation, prompting an emergency "DMCA Patch" that excised all Nintendo IP: Mario became "Infringio" (a parody placeholder character), levels were retiled with generics, and the game relaunched as "DMCA Royale" or "Infringio Royale" the same day.<grok:render card_id="18c743" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">
