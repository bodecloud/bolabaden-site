## Jessica Djukanovic's Case Timeline

Jessica and Miljan Djukanovic, a conservative, religious Iowa couple with four kids, learned of their pregnancy weeks before November 20, 2025.

At about 10 weeks, tests showed the fetus had Trisomy 18 (Edwards syndrome), a chromosomal issue multiple doctors deemed incompatible with life—no survival chance.

Jessica's health worsened: chest pain, swelling, fatigue, kidney problems. She described it as "a dying fetus poisoning me from the inside out."

Over more than two weeks post-diagnosis, multiple Iowa providers denied care. One suggested miscarrying at home or waiting; another advised online searches; others said "hands tied."

They fought for help, reaching Broadlawns ER, which coordinated with University of Iowa. She received treatment there, symptoms easing immediately.
