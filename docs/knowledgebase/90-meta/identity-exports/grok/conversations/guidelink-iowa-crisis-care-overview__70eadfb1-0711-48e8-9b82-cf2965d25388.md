## Overview
GuideLink Center is a 24/7 drop-in facility in Iowa City for adults 18+ in mental health or substance use crises. It's not an ER or jail—think of it as a middle ground that popped up because overloaded hospitals and lockups were dumping grounds for non-medical breakdowns. Before this, folks waited hours in ERs for psych evals or got arrested for public intoxication, burning cash on the wrong fixes. GuideLink cuts that by offering quick onsite triage without the chaos.

Located at 300 Southgate Avenue, it's a 23,500-square-foot spot with 16 beds for stays up to five days. Run by Community and Family Resources (formerly AbbeHealth under UnityPoint Health).

## What to Expect
Walk in or get dropped off—no appointment, open 365 days. Expect a 20-minute to 1.5-hour assessment by counselors. If needed, admission to one of three programs: crisis stabilization (for psych issues like suicidal thoughts), sobering (safe alcohol withdrawal monitoring), or detox (med-supervised drug pullback).

No major medical emergencies here—minor first aid only, plus telemedicine links to University of Iowa Hospitals ER. They'll hook you up with follow-ups: therapy, housing, jobs, peer support.
