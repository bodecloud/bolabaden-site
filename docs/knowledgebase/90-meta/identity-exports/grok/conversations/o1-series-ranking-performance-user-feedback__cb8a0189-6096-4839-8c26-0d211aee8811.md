**Comprehensive Overview of OpenAI's o1 Series Models**  
OpenAI's o1 series, introduced in 2024, marked a shift toward models trained specifically for chain-of-thought reasoning using reinforcement learning. Unlike traditional GPT models, o1 variants "think" step-by-step internally before responding, leading to superior performance on PhD-level science, competitive programming, and multi-step problems. The family includes the flagship o1, the cost-efficient o1-mini, and the initial o1-preview release. A "pro" tier or mode provided extra compute for even harder tasks. By late 2025, these were classified as legacy, with official documentation noting deprecation of o1-preview and o1-mini, and succession by advanced reasoning lines (o3/o4 briefly, then GPT-5 series).

Key historical strengths:  
- o1 scored in the 89th percentile on Codeforces, top-500 US student level on math olympiad qualifiers, and near-PhD performance on physics/chemistry/biology benchmarks.  
- o1-mini nearly matched o1 on many STEM evaluations while being significantly cheaper and faster.  

However, limitations included no native real-time web access initially, restricted multimodal capabilities (later added text+image), and high latency/cost due to extended internal reasoning.

**Detailed Benchmark and Leaderboard Analysis**  
Academic and specialized benchmarks from 2024 showed o1 dominating reasoning-heavy tests: high scores on GPQA (diamond), AIME math, and complex coding contests. o1-mini was close behind on math/coding but trailed on general knowledge.

**In-Depth User Feedback and Perceptions**  
Reddit and forum discussions from release period:  
- Positive: "Simply amazing," "feels different," "nails tough puzzles," superior to Claude 3.5/GPT-4o on reasoning chains. Users in coding, research, and education fields reported game-changing results.  
- Mixed/Negative: Complaints about response delays, token costs, no file uploads initially, and overthinking simple queries. Some found o1-mini more practical daily, while full o1 was reserved for "hardest problems."  
- Later views (2025+): As GPT-5, Gemini 3, Grok 4, and Claude 4.5 arrived, users noted o1 felt "broken" or limited by comparison in speed/consistency. DeepSeek R1 and other open models also drew favorable comparisons in specific tests.

Overall sentiment: Diplomatic respect for o1 as a pivotal advancement that enabled current frontiers, but consensus that it's no longer the go-to for most users.
