The question targets "dumbest" by IQ or test scores. IQ data across countries comes from patchy, often biased studies like those by Lynn, full of errors—think estimating entire nations from tiny, unrepresentative samples. Those old lists with scores in the 40s-50s got debunked for ignoring factors like nutrition, education access, and cultural bias in tests. They're not worth chasing unless you want junk science.

Test scores offer a cleaner metric. PISA (Programme for International Student Assessment) runs every three years by the OECD, testing 15-year-olds in math, science, and reading. Before PISA, countries relied on their own exams, which couldn't compare systems head-to-head—apples to oranges in spotting what works or fails in education globally. PISA fixes that with standardized tasks applied to about 600,000 kids per cycle, focusing on real-world application over rote learning.

Latest data is from 2022, covering 81 countries/economies. I averaged the three subjects for fairness. These are the bottom five by average score (lowest first). Low scores tie to poverty, underfunded schools, instability—human basics ignored.
