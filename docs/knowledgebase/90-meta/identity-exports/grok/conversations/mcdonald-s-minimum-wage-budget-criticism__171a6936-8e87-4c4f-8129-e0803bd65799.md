## Background

McDonald's put out this budget guide in 2013. It came from their employee resource website, done with Visa to push financial literacy. Before this, companies like them might have ignored worker finances or left it to generic bank advice. This one aimed to show budgeting on low pay, but instead highlighted how minimum wage falls short compared to ignoring real costs in past silence.

## The Budget Breakdown

It assumes two jobs for income: first job nets $1,105/month, second $955, total $2,060 after taxes.

Monthly expenses listed:

- Savings: $100
- Rent/mortgage: $600
- Car payment: $150
- Car insurance: $100
- Health insurance: $20
- Heating: $0
- Electric: $90
- Cable/phone: $100
- Other: $160

Leaves $800 for daily spending, or $27/day.

## Why It Got Mocked

People reposted it because it pretends you can live without eating or driving costs. Health at $20 ignores real premiums around $200 then. Zero heating assumes free warmth somehow. No childcare means no kids or magic free care.

Compared to no guide before, this "solved" nothing—exposed wage issues instead. Worth noting if studying corporate PR fails or wage debates; otherwise, skip for better econ sources.
