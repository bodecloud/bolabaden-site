**OpenKotOR Channel Disclaimer: For the Record**

For many years, the Knights of the Old Republic modding community followed strict rules against porting assets—such as models, textures, animations, or other files—between KOTOR 1 and KOTOR 2, or pulling unused content from console versions like the Xbox disc. These restrictions started on LucasForums (the main hub when the publisher still owned the forums) and carried over to DeadlyStream. The policies aimed to avoid any risk of upsetting LucasArts/Disney, prevent one game’s assets from being used by players who might not own both titles, and encourage fully original creations instead of mixing existing files. In practice, this created friction: talented people sometimes walked away from projects rather than rebuild something from scratch, and even restoration efforts like early M4-78 work quietly used console-extracted assets when such practices were still frowned upon or outright taboo.

Times have changed. The community eventually relaxed these old rules, recognizing that the two games run on the same engine and share a lot of DNA. Today, porting between the games is common and accepted in most places, as long as it stays non-commercial. OpenKotOR exists for exactly this reason: to let developers, modders, and enthusiasts freely experiment, share tools, extract unused vanilla assets (like certain droid models), and get things done without outdated gatekeeping or unnecessary drama. As long as nobody is making money off the work and everyone gives proper credit where due, there is no ethical or practical problem with moving assets around within the official games. This group is deliberately separate from any single forum or legacy organization. We are here to keep these classic games alive, improve them for modern play, and support open creativity in a different era—one focused on progress instead of prohibition.
