It originates in Victor Hugo's novel, where Gavroche sings it in multiple scenes (accounting for its use "in other spots"), and it was incorporated into the original 1980 French production of the musical as **"La Faute à Voltaire"**. **Je ne suis pas notaire,**  
**C'est la faute à Voltaire,**  
**Je suis un petit oiseau,**  
**C'est la faute à Rousseau.**

**English translation** (for clarity):  
I fell to the ground,  
It's Voltaire's fault,  
My nose in the gutter,  
It's Rousseau's fault.  

I'm not a notary,  
It's Voltaire's fault,  
I'm a little bird,  
It's Rousseau's fault.

This playful, satirical refrain humorously blames the philosophers Voltaire and Rousseau for personal and societal misfortunes, capturing Gavroche's defiant and cheeky spirit.
