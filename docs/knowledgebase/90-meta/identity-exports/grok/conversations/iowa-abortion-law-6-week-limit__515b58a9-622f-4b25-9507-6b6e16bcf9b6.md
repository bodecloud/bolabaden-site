### Iowa Abortion Policy as of Late 2025 (Applies to 2026)

Iowa's current law is a "fetal heartbeat" ban. It took effect July 29, 2024, after years of court blocks following the 2022 Dobbs decision that ended federal Roe protection. No major changes have occurred by December 2025. Debates are planned for the 2026 legislative session—some push for tighter restrictions (like on medication abortion), others for loosening—but the law remains unchanged heading into 2026.

### The Cutoff Point

You can no longer get a legal abortion once a fetal heartbeat is detected. This typically happens around 6 weeks of pregnancy (counted from last menstrual period). Many people don't realize they're pregnant by then. Before heartbeat detection, abortion is allowed.

After that point, most abortions are banned.

### Exceptions (Narrow)

- Medical emergency to save the pregnant person's life.
- Fatal fetal abnormality.
- Rape (must report to law enforcement or health agency within 45 days).
- Incest (within 140 days).

No broad exceptions for health, economics, or personal reasons.

### Practical Reality

Abortions in Iowa dropped sharply (over 70% in the first year). Many travel to states like Minnesota or Illinois. Clinics require ultrasound to check for heartbeat first.

This replaced the prior limit of about 20-22 weeks, which existed before the ban was upheld.
