## Assistant

Creating a small YouTube channel can act as therapy by:

- **Emotional release**: Scripting/talking through feelings reduces rumination.
- **Structure & purpose**: Weekly uploads give routine and dopamine hits.
- **Micro-community**: Even 10-50 loyal viewers provide validation and belonging.
- **Skill-building confidence**: Editing, thumbnails, and SEO mastery prove “I can improve.”

Practical low-pressure steps:
1. Make faceless/nameless videos (voice-over, gameplay, text-to-speech, pet cam).
2. Topic = your actual struggles (anxiety logs, ADHD hacks, loneliness diary).
3. Post 1 short (<60 sec) per day for 30 days — no editing needed.
4. Title honestly: “Day 12 of feeling empty” → attracts people who relate.
5. Read comments aloud to yourself; reply to every one → instant social exposure therapy.

Result: Consistent small wins + genuine human connection = cheaper than therapy, often more effective for creators with <1k subs. Start today; phone + free CapCut is enough.

Steps to start a small YouTube channel:  
1. Choose a niche based on your passions.  
2. Set up a free YouTube account and channel.  
3. Get basic gear (phone camera, mic).  
4. Create and upload simple videos (talk like to a friend).  
5. Optimize with titles, thumbnails, tags.  
6. Promote on social media.  

Therapeutic benefits:  
- Expresses emotions, reduces stress through creativity.  
- Builds community for support and connection.  
- Boosts self-confidence via feedback and achievements.
