**Detailed Examination of KOTOR II Versions on GOG**  

*Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II - The Sith Lords* (KOTOR II), developed by Obsidian Entertainment and originally published by LucasArts in 2004-2005, has seen multiple digital re-releases. The key distinction lies between the original "legacy" build—often called "legacypc" on platforms like Steam—and the updated version handled by Aspyr Media.  

Aspyr, known for porting classic Star Wars titles to modern systems, released their enhanced version starting in 2015. This update initially appeared on Steam, Mac App Store, and Linux platforms, adding features like native widescreen resolution support, controller compatibility, Steam achievements, and Workshop integration (where applicable). It also included bug fixes but introduced some new issues, sparking community debate.  

For years, GOG.com sold the unmodified original version, appealing to players who favored DRM-free releases and extensive modding (e.g., the popular *The Sith Lords Restored Content Mod* or TSLRCM). Older forum discussions from 2015-2021 highlight users preferring GOG precisely to avoid Aspyr's changes, which some viewed as buggy or less mod-friendly.  

This changed in 2022 when GOG integrated Aspyr's update as the default version. Authoritative sources like PCGamingWiki confirm: "GOG.com uses Aspyr's updated version ('1.0b update'), made available in 2022." The legacy original remains accessible through GOG Galaxy by selecting an older branch labeled "Version 1.0b" after disabling automatic updates. This mirrors Steam's setup, where the main branch is Aspyr's and "legacypc" is the beta for the original.  

The Aspyr version on GOG brings clear advantages for modern hardware: better resolution handling (though menus may stretch horizontally), improved controller support, and overall stability on current Windows systems. However, the original legacy version often gets recommended for heavy modders, as certain community patches and restorations install more reliably on the unmodified executable.  

In summary, if you purchase or download KOTOR II from GOG today, you receive the Aspyr version—the same modernized edition from the 2015-2017 period—unless you manually revert. This aligns GOG with other major platforms while preserving access to the classic build for purists.
