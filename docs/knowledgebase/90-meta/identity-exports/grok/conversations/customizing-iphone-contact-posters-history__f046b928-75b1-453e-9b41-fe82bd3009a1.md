### Problem Recap
Your iPhone pulls contact info (name, photo) from the sender's "My Card" when they share their number. This happens via iMessage/SMS integration with Contacts app. No global opt-out exists—it's automatic if the sender has a card set up.

### Who Controls It
**You do, as the person giving the number.**  
The receiver sees whatever is in *your* My Card. They can't edit it on their end without your input. If you don't set a card, they get just the number (or whatever they manually add).

### How to Customize
1. Open **Contacts** app on your iPhone.

## Feature Basics

Contact posters build on older iPhone contact photos, which were just small images tied to your Apple ID or shared manually. Before iOS 17, you'd set a basic photo in your contact card, and it might show in calls or messages if shared via iCloud. This solved anonymous caller IDs but stayed simple—no styles or effects. Posters add customization for full-screen display on calls, making identification faster versus plain text names.

## Full History

Contact sharing started basic in early iOS—manual entry or iCloud sync for names/photos since iOS 5 (2011). By iOS 10 (2016), photos appeared in calls if set in contacts app. iOS 17 added posters for visual flair on incoming calls, plus NameDrop for proximity sharing via AirDrop tech (introduced same time). No major changes until iOS 18 (2024), which let apps request specific contacts instead of full access, tightening privacy.<grok:render card_id="077366" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">

## How to Customize Your Poster

Open Contacts app.

Tap "My Card" at top.

Tap "Contact Photo & Poster."

Tap "Edit."

Swipe to select a poster (up to 4 slots).

Tap "Customize" on one.

Choose "Poster."

## Adjustable Elements

**Image/Visual:** Pick photo from library, camera, Memoji, or monogram (initials). Apply effects: Depth (background blur), Studio Light, Contour, Color Wash (tint), Duotone, Color Background.<grok:render card_id="f8fbbf" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">

## Sharing Controls

In "Contact Photo & Poster" screen:

Toggle "Name & Photo Sharing" on/off.

Under "Share Automatically": None (manual only), Contacts Only (auto with your contacts), Always Ask (prompt each time).<grok:render card_id="96a67a" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">

This controls if your poster auto-appears when you call/message others on iOS.

## Other Settings

Settings > Apps > Phone > My Contact Poster: Link to same edit screen.

Settings > Phone > Incoming Calls: Toggle "Show Contact Posters" for others' posters on your phone—not yours.

No deeper tweaks; it's tied to Apple ID for sync across devices.

## Worth Your Time?

Setup takes 2-5 minutes. If you call often and want quick recognition, yes—beats default gray silhouette. Skip if privacy matters more than looks; turning off sharing avoids it entirely. No better alternative in iOS; Android's version is newer but similar in effort.
