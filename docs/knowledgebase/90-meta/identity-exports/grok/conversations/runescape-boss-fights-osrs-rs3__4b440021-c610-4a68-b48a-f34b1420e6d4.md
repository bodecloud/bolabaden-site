### RuneScape 3 Biggest Boss Fights
RS3 bosses are often designed with intricate mechanics, high damage output, and scalability for solo or group play. Here are some of the most notable:

1. **Telos, the Warden**
   - **Location**: Heart of Gielinor
   - **Difficulty**: Extremely high, especially at high enrage (up to 4,000%)
   - **Why It’s Big**: Telos is a solo boss with scaling difficulty based on enrage, which increases after each kill. It has five phases with mechanics like beam alignment, minion management, and a devastating special attack (the "anima bomb"). At 100%+ enrage, it requires near-perfect prayer flicking and gear optimization.
   - **Loot**: Dormant Anima Core weapons, Reprisal codices, and rare drops like the Seren Godbow.
   - **Why Iconic**: It’s considered one of the hardest solo bosses, with a steep learning curve and prestige for high enrage kills (e.g., "Warden" title at 1,000% enrage).

2. **Solak**
   - **Location**: The Lost Grove
   - **Difficulty**: Very high
   - **Why It’s Big**: Solak is designed for 1-7 players (optimal for duos or small groups) and features eight phases with complex mechanics like blight damage, root bindings, and an anima storm. The fight demands coordination, with players managing adds, healing Solak’s core, and surviving massive AoE attacks.
   - **Loot**: Blightbound crossbows, Erethdor’s grimoire, and the Merethiel’s stave.
   - **Why Iconic**: Known as the "Guardian of the Grove," Solak was the hardest boss upon release in 2018, requiring precise teamwork and high DPS.

3. **Vorago**
   - **Location**: Borehole (near Falador)
   - **Difficulty**: High
   - **Why It’s Big**: Vorago is a group boss (1-10 players) with five phases, each introducing mechanics like gravity fields, team splits, and the infamous "green bomb" that can one-shot players if not handled correctly. Hard mode adds even tougher challenges.
   - **Loot**: Seismic wand, singularity, and Vitalis pet.
   - **Why Iconic**: A staple of high-level PvM since 2012, Vorago’s massive health pool (250,000+ in normal mode) and group coordination make it a legendary fight.

4. **Araxxor/Araxxi**
   - **Location**: Araxxor’s Lair (Morytania)
   - **Difficulty**: High, scales with enrage
   - **Why It’s Big**: A solo or duo spider boss with four phases, Araxxor’s difficulty increases with enrage (up to 300%) and varies based on daily path rotations (e.g., minion spawns, acid pools). Araxxi, the final phase, has devastating attacks like cleave and web shield.
   - **Loot**: Noxious weapons (scythe, bow, staff) and spider leg components.
   - **Why Iconic**: Its accessibility and scaling difficulty make it a favorite for both new and veteran PvMers, with a steep challenge at high enrage.

5. **Zamorak, Lord of Chaos**
   - **Location**: Senntisten (Zamorakian Undercity)
   - **Difficulty**: Extremely high at high enrage
   - **Why It’s Big**: Released in 2022, Zamorak is a solo or group boss with seven phases, featuring mechanics like rune management, infernal tomb, and a chaotic enrage stack system (up to 3,600% enrage). The final phase’s "Chaos Pentagram" is a brutal DPS check.
   - **Loot**: Bow of the Last Guardian, codices, and Zamorakian titles.
   - **Why Iconic**: Its lore significance (tied to the Elder God Wars) and extreme difficulty at high enrage make it a pinnacle PvM challenge.

### Old School RuneScape (OSRS) Biggest Boss Fights
OSRS bosses are generally simpler mechanically than RS3 but are iconic for their grind-heavy requirements, group dynamics, or solo prestige. Here are the biggest:

1. **The Nightmare of Ashihama**
   - **Location**: Morytania
   - **Difficulty**: High
   - **Why It’s Big**: Designed for 1-80 players (optimal for 4-8), the Nightmare has four phases with mechanics like totems, sleepwalking, and parasitic surges. Its attacks (e.g., "Husks" and "Grasping Claws") require constant movement and prayer switches.
   - **Loot**: Inquisitor’s mace, Nightmare staff, and Harmonised orb.
   - **Why Iconic**: Its scalability and valuable drops make it a popular mass boss, but small-team or solo kills are prestigious.

2. **Zulrah**
   - **Location**: Zul’Andra
   - **Difficulty**: Moderate to high (solo)
   - **Why It’s Big**: A solo-only snake boss with rotating attack patterns (ranged, magic, melee) and phases that force players to memorize its rotations (four main patterns). It requires precise movement, prayer switches, and gear swaps.
   - **Loot**: Tanzanite fang, Magic fang, and Zulrah’s scales.
   - **Why Iconic**: Zulrah is a cornerstone of OSRS PvM, known for its steep learning curve and consistent money-making potential.

3. **Theatre of Blood (Raids 2)**
   - **Location**: Morytania
   - **Difficulty**: Very high
   - **Why It’s Big**: A 4-5 player raid with six bosses (The Maiden, Bloat, Nylocas, Sotetseg, Xarpus, and Verzik Vitur). Each boss has unique mechanics, like dodging blood spawns, surviving maze runs, or managing team splits. Verzik’s final phase is a brutal test of endurance and coordination.
   - **Loot**: Scythe of Vitur, Justiciar armour
