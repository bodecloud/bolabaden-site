### ASB or SSB?

**ASB** (artificially sweetened beverage). Zero sugar, no calories from carbs. Not SSB (sugar-sweetened).

### Is it "good"?

Compared to sugary sodas, it's better if you're cutting calories or managing blood sugar short-term — no direct spikes, helps with weight if that matters to you.

Downsides: Sucralose is artificial. Studies show potential issues like reduced insulin sensitivity, gut bacteria changes, or higher cravings in some people.<grok:render card_id="19267b" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">

Worth your time? Only if regular soda is your alternative. Otherwise, skip processed drinks.

### Artificial Sweeteners and "Healthy"

No artificial sweetener is healthy.

They exist because people want sweet drinks without sugar's calories or blood sugar spikes — sugar causes clear weight gain and diabetes risk.

Artificial ones cut that, starting from old diet sodas using saccharin (cancer scares fixed later), then aspartame, now sucralose dominant.

All FDA-approved as safe: aspartame, sucralose, acesulfame K, saccharin, neotame, advantame.

Downsides exist in studies: aspartame possible cancer link, sucralose gut bacteria disruption, ace K less tested.

None add nutrition. They might increase cravings or mess metabolism indirectly.

If picking least bad artificial, sucralose has strong safety record in many reviews.<grok:render card_id="ee770d" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">
