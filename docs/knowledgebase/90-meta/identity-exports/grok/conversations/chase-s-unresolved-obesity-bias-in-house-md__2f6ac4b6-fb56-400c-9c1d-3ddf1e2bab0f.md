### Episode: "Heavy" (Season 1, Episode 16)

Patient is 10-year-old Jessica Simms, morbidly obese, collapses with heart attack in school gym class.

Chase pushes hard that her weight caused everything. During initial team discussion, he says the heart attack stems directly from obesity, dismisses other ideas. Cameron presents case to House; Chase interrupts, insists it's just her being fat.

Team examines her. Jessica refuses scale; Chase demands accurate weight for diagnosis. Foreman tries relating (says he was heavy as kid, grew out of it); Chase argues you don't "grow out" of obesity at 90 pounds, calls it unhealthy choice.

Tests run. Jessica gets angry, demanding. Team suspects hypoglycemia, sedates her. Chase keeps circling back to weight blaming.

House suspects diet pills. Team searches home; find evidence she shoplifted pills, exercised excessively. Chase still ties symptoms to obesity. Skin necrosis appears on chest; Chase links it to fat.

Necrosis worsens, requires removing breast tissue (devastating for child). Chase stays fixated on weight.

Near end, Chase shifts: suggests obesity is symptom, not cause. Ties to House's clue (patient short, mom tall—no dad history). Diagnosis: Cushing's syndrome from pituitary tumor causing weight gain, heart issues.

Key Chase moments:
- Blurts "She's fat!" when House presses for quick answer on error.
- Foreman snaps: "Enough, already, okay? We got it. You hate fat people."
- Chase: "That's not what I meant!" but doesn't back down fully.

Chase gets called out repeatedly, affects team arguments (mostly with Cameron/Foreman). Bias delays seeing weight as symptom. No explanation given for why he thinks this way. Episode ends with Jessica post-surgery, lost weight; Chase notes improvement positively.

House kicks Chase off case temporarily? No—bias influences but he stays involved until realization.

### Episode: "Que Será Será" (Season 3, Episode 6)

Patient is George Hagel, extremely obese (~600 pounds), found comatose in apartment after small fire. Firefighters assume dead initially, joke about weight.

Team takes case. Chase immediately judgmental: calls him suicidal for reaching that size, not just obese or morbidly obese—beyond that. Says people who attempt suicide get depressed, ties it to laziness/self-neglect.

Cameron/Foreman exchange looks, signal Chase's bias flaring again.

Tests/treatments run. George wakes briefly, complains symptoms. Chase remains critical, dismissive.

Bias impacts care: team/House sidelines Chase from direct involvement because he can't stay objective. He disappears from patient interactions mid-episode, focuses elsewhere (subplot with Cameron).

Diagnosis progresses without him heavily: turns out small cell lung cancer causing syndrome leading to coma/other issues (not weight primary).

Chase's comments highlight prejudice, get him removed from active case role. No deeper dive into why he reacts this way. Trait repeats from earlier episode but no resolution or backstory.
