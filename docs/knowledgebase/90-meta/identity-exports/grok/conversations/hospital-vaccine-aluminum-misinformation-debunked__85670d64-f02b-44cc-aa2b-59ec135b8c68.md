### Core Claim Breakdown
Your video likely cherry-picks a low FDA limit (4-5 mcg/kg/day) from IV feeding rules for premature or kidney-impaired infants. Hospitals follow CDC vaccine schedules, which use aluminum as an adjuvant (immune booster) in some shots like DTaP, HepB, Hib, and PCV. No direct FDA "contradiction"—different contexts, same agency. Thimerosal (mercury preservative)? Mostly gone from infant vaccines since 2001; trace amounts only. Focus here is aluminum, the common "metal" gripe.

### FDA Limits: What They Actually Cover
- **IV Nutrition (Parenteral)**: Caps at 4-5 mcg aluminum/kg body weight/day for premies or renal patients. Why? Direct bloodstream delivery, poor kidney clearance. A 5kg (11lb) 2-month-old: ~20-25 mcg/day max. Created post-1990s toxicity scares in hospital-fed kids; solved absorption issues in non-oral feeding vs. prior unchecked additives.
- **Vaccines (Injected)**: Per 21 CFR 610.15, max 0.85-1.25 mg (850-1250 mcg) *per dose*, not daily/kg. Set in 1968 (NIH, carried by FDA) for efficacy in toxoids like DTP—boosts antibody response without full antigen doses. Better than old whole-cell vaccines (weaker immunity, more shots needed). No cumulative infant limit; FDA models show first-year total (4.4 mg from all shots) stays under safe blood levels (< half minimal risk threshold).
- **Why Separate?** Injected aluminum absorbs slower (stays at muscle site), clears via kidneys in healthy babies. Oral/dietary aluminum (from formula/breast milk) barely absorbs (0.1-0.3%). Video ignores this—mixes IV rules with shots.

### Vaccine Aluminum: Real Numbers vs. Hype
Standard CDC schedule (birth-6 months): HepB (birth, 250 mcg), DTaP/Hib/PCV (2/4/6 months, 170-625 mcg each visit). Cumulative: ~4 mg total, spread out.
- Vs. IV limit: One DTaP dose (625 mcg) dwarfs 25 mcg for a 2-month-old—but irrelevant; that's not how vaccines work.
- Vs. diet: Breastfed baby gets 7 mg in 6 months; formula, 38 mg; soy, 117 mg. Shots add ~10% of that, but injected form clears faster in studies.
- Fact-checks (AFP, USA Today, FactCheck.org): Claims of "exceeding limits" flop—misapply IV rules. No toxicity spikes in vaccinated kids' blood/hair samples.

### The Other Side: Legit Gripes?
Some researchers (Lyons-Weiler 2018, Angrand 2024) argue FDA's per-dose cap ignores infant weight/kidney immaturity—newborns get 17x adult-adjusted dose Day 1. Models predict "toxicity days" (above provisional limits) up to 52% of first year on CDC schedule. No safety trials for cumulative injection in premies; limit based on 1960s efficacy, not tox data (FOIA confirmed: zero infant-specific studies). Objectively: Benefits (preventing whooping cough, etc.) outweigh modeled risks in population studies—no autism/chronic disease spikes post-aluminum use (70+ years). But if your kid's premature/renal? Delay/spread shots; docs can swap thimerosal-free flu versions.

## The Claim in the Video

## Where This Comes From

Aluminum in vaccines isn't new; it's been around since the 1930s. Before adjuvants, vaccines needed higher doses of killed pathogens to work, raising risks of side effects or production issues. Aluminum solved that by making weaker antigens effective, cutting costs and improving safety over older methods like no adjuvant at all.

The misinformation twists an FDA rule for IV fluids. That rule caps aluminum at 5 mcg/kg body weight per day for parenteral nutrition—IV feeding for sick preemies or kidney-impaired patients who can't eat normally. In those cases, aluminum builds up because it's directly in the bloodstream and hard to filter out.

Anti-vaxxers apply this IV limit to vaccines, calculate total aluminum from shots (like DTaP, Hep B at birth/2/6 months), and say it "overdoses" babies. Example: A post claims 3000 mcg in one day, but that's cherry-picking and wrong context.

## The Actual Reality

Vaccines don't follow the IV nutrition limit because they're not IV. Shots are intramuscular—aluminum releases slowly over weeks, not dumped all at once. Healthy babies' kidneys excrete 95% within days.

FDA's real vaccine rule: No more than 1.25 mg aluminum per dose. Total from full schedule in first 6 months? About 4.4 mg. Compare to diet: Breastfed babies get 7 mg, formula-fed 38 mg, soy formula 117 mg in same period. Vaccines add less than food.

Studies on over 1 million kids show no links to autism, asthma, or neuro issues from vaccine aluminum. It's safe because dose and route matter—unlike IV overload in vulnerable patients.

Hospitals follow CDC schedules, which FDA approves. No contradiction; the "limit" cited doesn't apply.

## How Much Truth Is There?

Barely any. It's a misrepresentation to scare people. The IV limit exists, but slapping it on vaccines ignores biology and regs. If you're weighing vaccines' worth: They prevent diseases that killed millions pre-vax era. Alternatives? None objectively better—delaying risks outbreaks. Worth the time if preventing measles or pertussis matters more than debunked fears.
