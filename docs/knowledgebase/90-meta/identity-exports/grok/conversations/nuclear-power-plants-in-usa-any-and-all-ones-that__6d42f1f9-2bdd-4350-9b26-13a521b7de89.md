# Nuclear Power Plants in the USA

Data pulled from World Nuclear Association database as of late 2025. Covers all commercial reactors. Current: 95 operating reactors at 55 plants (includes Palisades restart in October 2025, first in US history). Historical: 42 permanently shutdown reactors (many single-unit plants). Dates use grid connection for start (proxy for commercial operation); shutdown dates where applicable. Grouped by plant for multi-unit sites. Capacities in net MWe.

Why track this? Nuclear fills baseload gaps coal/gas couldn't reliably without emissions—built post-WWII for energy independence, but shutdowns spiked from 1970s oil shocks, accidents like Three Mile Island, and cheap gas. Now restarting as climate push overrides economics.
