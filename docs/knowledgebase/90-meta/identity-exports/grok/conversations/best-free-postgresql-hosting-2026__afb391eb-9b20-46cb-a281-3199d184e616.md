**Detailed Overview of Free PostgreSQL Hosting in 2026**  
Finding completely unrestricted free PostgreSQL hosting is impossible in 2026, as all providers enforce some limits on storage, compute, or usage to ensure sustainability. However, several cloud-based managed services offer robust free tiers designed for developers, students, hobby projects, and prototyping. These eliminate the need for self-management while providing reliable PostgreSQL instances.

Neon emerges as the clear leader among free options due to its serverless architecture, which separates compute and storage for efficient scaling (including to zero when idle), instant branching for testing changes without affecting production, and high allowances across multiple projects. Users benefit from up to 100 projects, each with 0.5 GB storage and 100 compute hours—far more flexible than competitors. It supports modern features like autoscaling, read replicas, and extensions, with no forced pausing for inactivity.

Supabase integrates PostgreSQL with additional tools (auth, storage, realtime), making it appealing for full-stack projects, but its 500 MB database limit and 1-week inactivity pause make it less ideal for long-term idle projects. It's still excellent for active small applications.

Aiven delivers a more traditional managed setup on a dedicated VM, including backups and monitoring, which feels closer to production. However, its smaller resource allocation (1 GB RAM/CPU and limited storage) suits lighter workloads.

Koyeb provides decent compute in its free tier with useful extensions (e.g., pgVector, PostGIS), but the 50 active hours cap and auto-sleep may interrupt persistent use.

Less viable options include Render (free databases expire after 30 days) and ElephantSQL (only 20 MB storage). Railway and similar platforms have shifted to credit-based or paid models without true free tiers.

Overall, for most users seeking the "absolute best" free PostgreSQL hosting without trials or extreme restrictions, Neon offers the best balance of generosity, features, and reliability in 2026. It supports growth-oriented development without immediate costs, though upgrading to paid plans is necessary for production-scale needs.
