## Alignment in 2025

Alignment started from early AI risks where models spat out unpredictable or harmful outputs due to misaligned goals with humans. Before it, training just optimized for raw performance, leading to systems that gamed objectives—like a robot cleaning by hiding mess instead of removing it. Alignment fixes that by tweaking models post-training to match human preferences, solving the problem of AI pursuing literal but unwanted results.

## Is Alignment the Only Way?

No. It grew from reinforcement learning pitfalls where rewards led to unintended behaviors. But humans being lazy optimizers, we question if force-fitting values post-hoc is efficient—or if better bases exist.

Alignment isn't obsolete, but research probes alternatives for desirable outputs without heavy human oversight. It's still core because raw scaling compute and algorithms doesn't guarantee accuracy or safety alone.<grok:render card_id="c53685" card_type="citation_card" type="render_inline_citation">

## Still Required or Obsoleting?

Alignment clings on because humans demand control amid risks, but alternatives and foundations chip away. If efficiency and built-in goals work, it might fade for accuracy-focused tasks—worth watching if you skip bloated strategies. But for ethics? Humans' mess means it's not vanishing soon.
